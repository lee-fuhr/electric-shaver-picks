Electric Shaver questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

Electric shavers come in two main types, foil and rotary, and the best choice depends on your hair type, skin sensitivity, and shaving habits. Foil models work well for straight-growing, fine hair and sensitive skin, while rotary shavers excel at navigating curves and longer or coarser stubble. This guide answers the most common questions to help you choose, use, and maintain an electric shaver for a comfortable, close shave every time.

What’s the difference between foil and rotary shavers?

Foil shavers use a thin, oscillating blade behind a perforated metal screen (the foil). They cut hair in a straight, back-and-forth motion and are generally gentler on sensitive skin. You’ll find they work best if you have fairly straight hair that grows in a predictable direction, and they tend to give a very close finish on cheeks and neck. Rotary shavers have two or three circular blades that rotate beneath floating heads. They’re designed to follow the contours of your face and chin more naturally, making them a strong pick if you have a pronounced jawline or curly/wiry hair that grows in multiple directions. Rotary heads can handle longer stubble a bit better than foils, but they may require a little more pressure to get a smooth result. Neither type is universally better, it comes down to your hair texture and shaving style. Many well-known brands like Braun (foil) and Philips (rotary) offer excellent models across a range of budgets, so it’s worth reading owner feedback to see which design has worked for people with similar hair and skin to yours.

How do I get a closer shave with an electric shaver?

Prep matters more than most people think. Shave after a warm shower or wash your face with warm water to soften the hairs. If your shaver is dry-only, make sure your skin is completely dry, water can cause irritation and poor glide. For wet/dry models, using a pre-shave lotion or gel can help the heads glide and reduce drag, which often leads to a closer result. Technique is equally important. Use short, circular motions (especially with rotary shavers) and stretch your skin taut with your free hand. Avoid pressing hard; let the heads do the work. Many premium shavers now have flexible heads that follow your face, but you still need to go over the same area from different angles to catch hairs that lie flat. A clean shaver also cuts better, rinse the heads after every use and replace the foil or blades per the manufacturer’s recommendation (typically every 12 to 18 months).

Are electric shavers good for sensitive skin?

Yes, many electric shavers are specifically designed for sensitive skin, and they can actually be less irritating than a manual razor because they don’t cut as close to the skin’s surface. Foil shavers are often the top recommendation for sensitive skin because the oscillating blade is less likely to catch or tug, and the foil protects against nicks. Rotary shavers have also improved in recent years, with models that feature micro-vibrations to lift hair before cutting. Look for features like hypoallergenic foils, rounded cutter heads, and an adjustable speed setting. Shaving dry can be gentler than wet shaving for some, but try both routines to see which your skin tolerates best. A good pre-shave powder or alcohol-free balm afterwards can also calm irritation. Reputable brands like Panasonic and Braun have models that are widely praised by users with sensitive skin.

How often should I replace the blades or foil?

Most manufacturers recommend replacing the foil and blade assembly every 12 to 18 months, or sooner if you notice a decline in shaving performance, like more pulling, tugging, or a less close result. Some shavers have a light or indicator that tells you when it’s time, but it’s a good habit to check the condition of the foil every few months. Hold it up to the light and look for tiny holes or dull spots; if you see any, it’s time to swap. Using a shaver with a worn foil can lead to skin irritation and a less efficient shave. Replacement parts are widely available for most popular models from Braun, Philips, and Panasonic. They can be a few tens of dollars, so budgeting for these consumables is part of the total cost of ownership.

Should I shave wet or dry?

It depends on your shaver and personal preference. Wet/dry shavers are sealed so they can be used with shaving cream, gel, or foam in the shower. Wet shaving can provide more glide and comfort, especially if you have sensitive skin or very coarse hair. It also makes cleanup easier, you can rinse the shaver under a tap. Dry shaving is faster and more convenient, no need to prep or rinse foam, and you can shave without a mirror if you’re in a hurry. The trade-off is that dry shaving may cause a little more friction and can be less close. Try both approaches with a wet/dry model to see which gives you the smoothest, most irritation-free result. Some modern wet/dry shavers are designed to perform exceptionally well in both modes.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use an electric shaver every day?

Yes, most electric shavers are designed for daily use. In fact, many people find that shaving every day with an electric model is gentler than letting stubble grow for two or three days, because the hairs stay shorter and less likely to be pulled.

How do I clean an electric shaver?

For dry-only shavers, use the included brush to remove hair clippings from the blades and foil. For wet/dry models, rinse the head under warm running water. Most mid-range and premium shavers also come with a cleaning station that uses a cartridge of alcohol-based cleaner to sanitize and lubricate the blades automatically.

What’s the best electric shaver for a budget?

You don’t need to spend a lot for a good shave. Brands like Philips, Remington, and Braun offer reliable models under a few hundred dollars that perform well for daily grooming. Focus on features like a pop-up trimmer, a comfortable grip, and a battery that lasts through a week of shaves rather than extra bells and whistles.

Do electric shavers work on long beards or heavy stubble?

Most electric shavers are designed for hair lengths up to about half an inch. For longer beards, you’ll want to use a beard trimmer first to shorten the hair, then follow up with the shaver for a close finish. Many shavers include a built-in pop-up trimmer for this purpose.

Can I travel with an electric shaver?

Yes, and it’s one of the big advantages of electric shavers. Most models have a lock feature to prevent accidental activation, and many come with a travel case. Just be aware of the battery, if it’s a lithium-ion model, TSA allows it in carry-on luggage as long as it’s under a certain watt-hour rating (which nearly all consumer shavers meet).

In shortChoosing the right electric shaver comes down to understanding your own hair type, skin sensitivity, and routine. Foil and rotary each have their strengths, and the best way to decide is to read user experiences from people with similar needs to yours. Keep the shaver clean, replace the blades when they dull, and experiment with wet vs. dry shaving until you find what feels best. A good electric shaver should make your morning routine faster and more comfortable, not more complicated.
